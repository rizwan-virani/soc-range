01 How this works
Two ways to train

Skill labs build one technique at a time, with instant grading: write Sigma rules, run real SPL, make calls under a timer, or capture flags. Shift scenarios drop you into a full incident end to end, where you investigate a live SIEM, work the playbook, and produce the deliverables. New here? Warm up with a lab, then run a Tier 1 shift.

  1. Sign in above so your completion codes are tagged to you.
  2. Warm up in a skill lab. The Detection Lab and Live Response Simulator are the fastest way to learn the tools.
  3. Run a Tier 1 shift. Read the brief, investigate the feed, work the playbook, and submit. Each shift opens with a guided orientation you can replay anytime.
  4. Complete the written deliverables. Download the Word templates from the Reference Library, fill them with your screenshots and notes, and turn them in with your recording.
  5. Climb the tiers. Move from triage to incident response, threat hunting, advanced threats, and GRC as you build skill.

Multi-screen & classroom use

For classroom displays, open the Analyst Workstation and use the panel rail to move between screens. For projector setups, run one screen per monitor by opening multiple browser windows at the same scenario. This platform is built for a desktop or laptop with Chrome; the labs and feeds need a mouse and keyboard.

What this is

  • A hands-on SOC analyst trainer. You triage a live SIEM feed, work real incidents, and write the deliverables a working analyst produces.
  • Built for CYBR 4350. Every finished shift gives a completion code you turn in to Blackboard.
  • Self-paced and saved to this browser. Pick any tier, any time, and come back where you left off.

What this is not

  • Not a real network. Every host, log, and attacker is simulated for training. No live systems are touched.
  • Not a hacking tool. The focus is defense, detection, and response, not offense.
  • Not graded by clicks alone. The in-app work is step one of five. The written deliverables are the rest.
